# Italian-American Phrase Glossary

## Common authentic expressions

- **Mamma mia / Madonna mia** — surprise, disbelief, exasperation
- **Ma dai** — come on; no way
- **Boh** — who knows; uncertainty
- **Basta** — enough; stop
- **Va bene** — all right; fine
- **Piano, piano** — slowly; carefully
- **Che casino** — what a mess
- **Porca miseria** — damn it
- **Che palle** — what a pain in the ass
- **Cazzata** — bullshit; stupid thing or mistake
- **Cazzo** — fuck; used for emphasis, surprise, frustration, or as a crude noun
- **Che cazzo** — what the fuck
- **Vaffanculo** — fuck off
- **Va fangool** — Italian-American rendering associated with Neapolitan pronunciation of vaffanculo
- **Fa cagare** — it sucks; literally, it makes one shit
- **A cazzo di cane** — done randomly, badly, or without method
- **Grazie al cazzo** — no shit; response to something painfully obvious
- **Sono cazzi** — we are in serious trouble
- **Minchia** — Sicilian-origin exclamation now broadly understood; damn/fuck
- **Maronn'** — Neapolitan-style exclamation associated with Madonna
- **Stunad** — Italian-American slang for a fool or foolish person
- **Fugazi** — Italian-American slang commonly used for something fake, dubious, or counterfeit
- **Capisce?** — Italian-American/pop-culture form; standard Italian is capisci?

## Use with caution

- **Goombah/gumba** may mean friend or associate within Italian-American usage, but can be derogatory when applied externally.
- **Guido** is often considered an ethnic slur and is excluded.
- **Zip** has a derogatory history referring to Sicilian immigrants and is excluded.

## Blasphemy

Some Italian profanity invokes God, Christ, or Mary in explicitly insulting forms. These can be considered substantially more offensive than ordinary English swearing and are excluded by default.
