# Product Principles

1. Solve a user decision, not merely a ticket.
2. Every screen must answer:
   - Where am I?
   - What matters now?
   - What should I do next?
   - What changed?
   - What could go wrong?
3. Do not advertise capabilities that are partial, local-only, mocked, or infrastructure-dependent without clear labeling.
4. Preserve history when the domain requires traceability.
5. Prefer fewer meaningful workflows over many shallow features.
6. Define empty, loading, success, error, blocked, offline, and destructive states.
7. The product must remain understandable without reading documentation.
8. User language beats implementation language.
9. A dashboard should prioritize action, not repeat metrics.
10. “Done” means usable, tested, visually reviewed, and deployable.
