# Knowledge: Business & Leadership

Domain-specific knowledge base for dispatches and discussions about leadership, decision-making, organizational dynamics, and operational excellence.

## Key Frameworks

### Kahneman — Thinking Fast and Slow
- **System 1 vs System 2** — automatic vs deliberative thinking
- **Anchoring bias** — first numbers anchor subsequent estimates
- **Loss aversion** — losses hurt 2x more than equivalent gains feel good
- **Planning fallacy** — we systematically underestimate time and cost
- **Pre-mortem** — imagine the project failed, then explain why. More effective than brainstorming risks.

### W. Edwards Deming — Continuous Improvement
- **PDCA cycle** — Plan-Do-Check-Act, the original iterative improvement loop
- **System over blame** — 94% of problems are system problems, not people problems
- **Profound knowledge** — appreciation for a system, knowledge of variation, theory of knowledge, psychology
- **Drive out fear** — people won't report problems if they're punished for problems

### Jack Welch — Operational Excellence
- **"Fix it, sell it, or close it"** — ruthless prioritization
- **Differentiation** — top 20%, vital 70%, bottom 10%. Controversial but clear.
- **Boundarylessness** — breaking down silos, best idea wins regardless of source
- **Speed** — "If you're not moving fast, you're not winning"

### Peter Drucker — Effectiveness
- **Effectiveness > efficiency** — doing the right things > doing things right
- **Time audit** — know where your time actually goes, not where you think it goes
- **Strategic abandonment** — what are we doing that we should stop doing?
- **The manager's job** — to make people productive, not to manage them

### Andy Grove — High Output Management
- **High leverage activities** — things where a small input creates large output
- **OKRs** — Objectives and Key Results, measurable goals that drive focus
- **Strategic inflection points** — moments when the fundamentals change
- **"Only the paranoid survive"** — vigilance against complacency

## Patterns That Hold

1. **What gets measured gets managed.** But only measure what matters. Vanity metrics are worse than no metrics.

2. **Culture eats strategy for breakfast.** You can have the best plan and the worst execution if the culture doesn't support it.

3. **The bottleneck is always somewhere.** Find it. Fix it. Repeat. This is the Theory of Constraints in practice.

4. **Most organizational problems are communication problems.** Unclear expectations, misaligned incentives, missing feedback loops.

5. **The best leaders create other leaders, not followers.** Teaching people to make decisions independently scales. Making all decisions yourself doesn't.

6. **Revenue cures most ills.** In early-stage businesses, the answer to most problems is "sell more." Optimization comes after survival.

## Connections to Other Domains

## Dispatch Themes

When generating dispatches in this domain:
- Apply frameworks to the user's specific business context
- Draw connections between business theory and real operational decisions
- Reference specific failures/successes as case studies
- Challenge conventional business wisdom when evidence contradicts it
- Connect personal productivity to organizational effectiveness
